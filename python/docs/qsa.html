qsa.analysis.spectralize(z)[source]¶

Compute discrete Fourier transform of a real signal. The FFT is normalized by the length of the signal. In particular, \(cos(t)\) is transformed to a sum of two complex exponentials with half amplitude

\[cos(t) = \frac{1}{2} e^{i t} + \frac{1}{2} e^{- i t}\]
Parameters:z (numpy.ndarray) – signal as a time series of real values
Returns:array of Fourier coefficients (complex numbers)
Return type:numpy.ndarray 
>>> t = numpy.linspace(0, 10, 10000)
>>> z = numpy.cos(2 * numpy.pi * t)
>>> z_sp = qsa.analysis.spectralize(z)
>>> numpy.max(numpy.abs(z_sp))
0.5000241787423103
qsa.analysis.unspectralize(z_sp)[source]¶

Compute inverse of a Fourier transform obtained with spectralize(). By definition, unspectralize(spectralize(z)) equals z.

Parameters:z_sp (numpy.ndarray) – a Fourier transform obtained with spectralize()
Returns:a real signal for which Fourier transform is z_sp
Return type:numpy.ndarray 
>>> t = numpy.linspace(0, 10, 10000)
>>> z = numpy.cos(2 * numpy.pi * t)
>>> z_sp = qsa.analysis.spectralize(z)
>>> z_sp_inv = qsa.analysis.unspectralize(z_sp)
>>> numpy.max(z - z_sp_inv)
7.771561172376096e-16

All frequencies, equivalent to LINEAR, QUADRATIC

Diagonal (harmonic) frequencies \(2 f_i\)

Linear frequencies \(f_i\)

MINUS = 61440¶

Difference frequencies \(|f_i - f_j|\)

PLUS = 3840¶

Sum frequencies \(f_i + f_j\)

QUADRATIC = 65520¶

Quadratic frequencies, equivalent to DIAGONAL, PLUS, MINUS

qsa.domain.hertz2fick(dt, duration, frequency)[source]¶

Convert a frequency given in hertz to the corresponding FFT index. Note that negative frequencies are converted to the right of positive frequencies.

Parameters:
  • dt (float) – sampling time (seconds)
  • duration (float) – experiment duration (seconds) such that \(1 / duration\) represents the minimal frequency
  • frequency (float) – frequency (hertz) to convert
Returns:

index of the frequency in the FFT
Return type:

float

 
>>> qsa.domain.hertz2fick(0.001, 10, 1)
10
>>> qsa.domain.hertz2fick(0.001, 10, -1)
9990
